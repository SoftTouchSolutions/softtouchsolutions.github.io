Can I run the Personal Tutor from the hard disk of my computer?

Yes. Copy the entire contents of the CD-ROM to a suitable folder on your hard-disk. The Personal Tutor requires about 55 MB of hard disk space. Start the Personal Tutor by navigating to its folder using Windows Explorer and double-clicking on the file 'PersonalTutor.htm'. This will open up your default browser. Bookmark the Personal Tutor start page in your browser just as you would an internet website.

By doing this the Personal Tutor will not be 'installed' in the technical sense on your computer, that is, you will not find an entry for it in the 'Add and Remove Software' utility. To completely remove the Personal Tutor from your computer simply delete the entire file structure from the folder into which you copied it.

Important note 1: The copyright and licence agreement for the Personal Tutor allows you to keep a copy of the Personal Tutor on the hard disk of your computer. This copy is for personal use only: you are NOT allowed to install the Personal Tutor in a multi-user environment (such as on a file server). Refer to the Licence Agreement and Copyright Statement.

Important note 2: Only copy and run authentic versions of the Personal Tutor. If you have any doubts about the authenticity of the Personal Tutor program DO NOT USE IT. Palgrave Macmillan Ltd cannot accept liability for damage resulting from the use of copies of the Personal Tutor that have been modified or contain malicious code. See Browser warning.

What is the relationship of the Personal Tutor and the Engineering Mathematics book?

The Personal Tutor contains a subset of the exercises contained in the book Engineering Mathematics 6th edition. It is designed as a complement to the book and not as a stand-alone product.

The questions in the Personal Tutor are broken down into steps. The Personal Tutor does not contain the explanatory material that is in the book, but each step of a question gives a reference to the relevant explanatory 'frame(s)' in the book.

The book Engineering Mathematics 6th edition is an essential requirement for the effective use of the Personal Tutor as a learning tool.  [Top]

Why are there only odd-numbered questions on the Personal Tutor?

The question numbers refer to all the odd-numbered Quiz, Test exercise and Further problem sections from the textbook Engineering Mathematics 6th edition, by K. A. Stroud with additions by Dexter J. Booth.  [Top]

Why are there no questions for Programme F.4 Graphs?

The Graphs topic does not lend itself to the Personal Tutor software.  [Top]

What are  Frames?

These refer you to the numbered frames in the equivalent Programme in the Engineering Mathematics 6th edition textbook. If you can't do the question and the Hint and Full working aren't helping, refer back to the book for more help.  [Top]

Where can I buy Engineering Mathematics 6th edition by K. A. Stroud with additions by Dexter J. Booth?

It is stocked in most university bookshops, is through online bookstores or direct from the publishers.  [Top]

What does the tick symbol in the question menu mean?

A tick symbol opposite a question entry means that you have completed that question by working through all the steps it contains. It isn't a score: it doesn't mean that you answered all or even any of the questions correctly. See Using the Personal Tutor: Choosing a question.  [Top]

My browser gave me a warning that the Personal Tutor may not be safe to use. What do I do?

The Personal Tutor uses JavaScript scripts, a technique that is used extremely widely in web pages. Your browser may be set to warn you when a page contains such scripts or even block them completely. You can trust the scripts in the Personal Tutor as long as (1) you are running the program from a genuine CD-ROM that you obtained as part of your purchase of Engineering Mathematics 6th edition or (2) you are running the Personal Tutor either directly from the online Stroud Personal Tutor website or (3) you are running the program from a download you obtained directly from the Stroud website.

If you have any doubts about the authenticity of the Personal Tutor program DO NOT USE IT. Palgrave Macmillan Ltd cannot accept liability for damage resulting from the use of copies of the Personal Tutor that have been modified or contain malicious code.   [Top]

Why won't the Personal Tutor start automatically when I put the CD-ROM in the drive?

The CD-ROM 'autorun' feature may have been disabled on your computer. See Installing and running the Personal Tutor.  [Top]

Do I need to have cookies enabled in my browser?

Cookies are not essential: the Personal Tutor will run correctly without them, but the record of the questions you have done (the tick symbols in the question menus) will be lost when you close or refresh the browser or navigate away from the program. This record will be stored between sessions if you have cookies enabled. Whether you are using the CD-ROM version or the website version of the Personal Tutor, no information from the cookie is sent to Palgrave Macmillan Ltd.  [Top]

How do I clear all the tick symbols?

Delete the cookie. How you do this depends on the browser you are using. Consult the documentation for your browser.  [Top]

If other people use my computer, will this change the record of the questions I have done?

If each person using the computer has and uses their own username then your records will be unaffected by anything the other users do in the Personal Tutor. If, on the other hand, all users of the computer are 'guests' without their own username, or if other people login under your username, then what they do will affect your records.
