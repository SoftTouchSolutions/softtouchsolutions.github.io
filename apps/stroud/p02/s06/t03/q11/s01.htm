Show that if a, b and c lie in the same plane then a · (b × c) = 0.
The vector product  b  ×  c  is, by definition, perpendicular to both  b  and  _ .
Therefore b × c is also perpendicular to any other vector
that lies in the plane containing  b  and  _ .
Since  _  also lies in this plane we can say that  a · ( b  ×  c )  =  0
because the scalar product of two perpendicular vectors is zero.
The scalar product of two mutually perpendicular vectors is zero.
